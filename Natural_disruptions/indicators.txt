HYDRO-CLIMATE INDICATORS
========================

These six indicators are extracted for every flood event (and matched non-flood
baseline samples) to test whether they can discriminate flood conditions from
normal conditions. All values are taken from the period immediately before or
during the event start date.

Data source: Google Earth Engine (GEE) unless noted otherwise.


SPI-30  (spi_30d)
-----------------
Standardised Precipitation Index over the 30 days before the event.
Computed from CHIRPS daily rainfall using a climatological baseline (1991-2020).
A z-score: 0 = average, +1 = one standard deviation wetter than normal.
Positive values indicate anomalously wet conditions preceding the flood.
AUC = 0.65 — moderate discriminator.
Flood mean: +0.58 vs baseline mean: +0.02


CHIRPS 7-day rainfall anomaly  (chirps_7d_anom_pct)
----------------------------------------------------
Total rainfall in the 7 days before the event, expressed as a percentage
anomaly relative to the climatological 7-day mean for that calendar window
(1991-2020 baseline). Derived from CHIRPS (~5.5 km resolution).
+100% means twice the normal rainfall; -100% means no rain at all.
Averaged over a 25 km buffer around the event location.
AUC = 0.67 — moderate discriminator.
Flood median: +18% vs baseline median: -37%


ERA5 soil moisture  (era5_soil_moisture_day0)
---------------------------------------------
Volumetric water content of the top soil layer (0-7 cm) on the event start
date, from ERA5-Land reanalysis (~9 km resolution). Units: m³/m³ (fraction
of soil volume occupied by water). Values near 0 = dry, near 0.5 = saturated.
High soil moisture indicates the ground was already wet before the flood,
reducing its capacity to absorb further rainfall.
AUC = 0.72 — strong discriminator.
Flood mean: 0.37 vs baseline mean: 0.27


GPM peak 3-hour intensity  (gpm_peak_3h_mm)
--------------------------------------------
Maximum rainfall recorded in any 3-hour window in the 7 days before the event,
from the GPM IMERG half-hourly product (~10 km resolution). Units: mm.
Captures extreme short-duration rainfall bursts that trigger flash floods,
which are missed by daily accumulation metrics.
AUC = 0.74 — strongest discriminator.
Flood median: 10.0 mm vs baseline median: 2.4 mm


JRC surface water recurrence  (jrc_recurrence_pct)
---------------------------------------------------
Percentage of years (1984-2021) in which open water was observed at the event
location, from the JRC Global Surface Water dataset (Landsat, 30 m resolution).
100% = permanently flooded (river/lake); 0% = never flooded historically.
Most events are near rivers so both floods and baseline samples score high,
which explains its poor discriminability.
AUC = 0.50 — essentially no discriminative power.
Flood mean: 86% vs baseline mean: 85%


Population density  (pop_density_km2)
--------------------------------------
Resident population per km² at the event location, from WorldPop (~100 m).
Not a hydro-climate variable — included as a proxy for exposure and reporting
bias (high-density areas are more likely to be reported as flood events).
AUC = 0.73 — strong discriminator, but driven by reporting bias rather than
physical flood conditions. Flood events cluster in populated areas.
Flood median: 89 /km² vs baseline median: 12 /km²


VALIDATION SUMMARY
==================

AUC (Area Under ROC Curve): probability that the indicator ranks a random
flood event higher than a random non-flood sample. 0.5 = random, 1.0 = perfect.

KS statistic: maximum separation between the flood and baseline cumulative
distributions. Higher = more separated distributions.

Indicator              AUC    KS
-------------------------------
gpm_peak_3h_mm        0.74  0.38
pop_density_km2       0.73  0.35
era5_soil_moisture    0.72  0.37
chirps_7d_anom_pct    0.67  0.26
spi_30d               0.65  0.23
jrc_recurrence_pct    0.50  0.10  <- not useful

All p-values are ~0 (KS test), confirming the distributions are statistically
different for all indicators except jrc_recurrence_pct (p=2e-6, marginal).
