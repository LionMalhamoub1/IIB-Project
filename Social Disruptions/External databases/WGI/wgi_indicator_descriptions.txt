WGI – Indicator Descriptions and Scaling
Source: Worldwide Governance Indicators (WGI), World Bank

---------------------------------------------------------------------

OVERVIEW

The Worldwide Governance Indicators (WGI) measure six dimensions of governance 
for over 200 countries worldwide.

Each indicator is:

- Annual
- Perception-based (constructed from multiple surveys and expert assessments)
- Standardised to allow cross-country comparison

These indicators are best interpreted as structural governance measures,
not short-term economic indicators.

---------------------------------------------------------------------

SCALING

The primary series used in modelling is the "Estimate" (.EST) version.

Scale:

- Approximately -2.5 = Weak governance
- Approximately +2.5 = Strong governance
- 0 ≈ Global average

Important notes:

- The scale is standardised (not a percentage).
- A difference of 0.5–1.0 is meaningful.
- Large year-to-year changes are rare.
- The scale is relative across countries and time.

Alternative versions available in the dataset:

- Percentile Rank (.PER.RNK): 0 to 100 scale
- Standard Error (.STD.ERR): measurement uncertainty

For modelling purposes, the EST series is typically preferred.

---------------------------------------------------------------------

THE SIX GOVERNANCE DIMENSIONS

1. Voice and Accountability (VA)

Measures:
- Citizen participation in government selection
- Freedom of expression
- Freedom of association
- Media freedom

Higher values → Strong political freedoms and democratic participation  
Lower values → Restricted civil liberties and limited participation

---------------------------------------------------------------------

2. Political Stability and Absence of Violence (PV)

Measures:
- Likelihood of political instability
- Risk of politically motivated violence
- Risk of terrorism

Higher values → Stable political environment  
Lower values → High instability or violence risk

This dimension is highly relevant for social disruption modelling.

---------------------------------------------------------------------

3. Government Effectiveness (GE)

Measures:
- Quality of public services
- Civil service independence
- Policy implementation capacity
- Government credibility

Higher values → Strong state capacity  
Lower values → Weak administrative effectiveness

Relevant for understanding shock absorption capacity.

---------------------------------------------------------------------

4. Regulatory Quality (RQ)

Measures:
- Government ability to design and implement sound policies
- Support for private sector development
- Market-friendly regulatory framework

Higher values → Predictable, business-friendly regulation  
Lower values → Distortive or unstable regulation

Less directly related to short-term unrest.

---------------------------------------------------------------------

5. Rule of Law (RL)

Measures:
- Confidence in legal institutions
- Property rights protection
- Contract enforcement
- Police and judicial effectiveness

Higher values → Strong legal institutions  
Lower values → Weak legal enforcement

Primarily structural rather than short-term predictive.

---------------------------------------------------------------------

6. Control of Corruption (CC)

Measures:
- Extent to which public power is used for private gain
- Both petty and grand corruption
- State capture by elites

Higher values → Low corruption  
Lower values → High corruption

Strongly linked to grievance-based unrest.

---------------------------------------------------------------------

MODELLING NOTES

For social disruption likelihood modelling:

Most relevant dimensions:
- Political Stability (PV)
- Government Effectiveness (GE)
- Control of Corruption (CC)

WGI should be used as a structural conditioning variable,
not as a high-frequency trigger.
